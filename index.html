Chapter 4: Stress & Emphasis

4 of 6

When we speak, we naturally place stress on certain words and syllables. This stress pattern helps listeners understand the most important information in a sentence.


Key Concepts:

  • Word Stress – Emphasizing the correct syllable in a word (e.g., RE-cord vs re-CORD)
  • Sentence Stress – Highlighting the most important word in a sentence
  • Contrastive Stress – Using stress to show contrast between ideas

Example: "I never said she stole the money" vs "I never said she stole the money"

Notice how stressing different words changes the entire meaning!


Practice reading aloud, paying close attention to which words feel naturally emphasized. Record yourself and compare with native speakers to improve your stress patterns.
